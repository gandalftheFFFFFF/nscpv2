About

My name is Niels and I'm a student at the IT-University of Copenhagen (ITU), where I study Software Development and technology. I graduate in the summer of 2016 after I have written my thesis on big data and energy management. During my studies at ITU I have specialised in business analytics, where I have taken courses such as data mining, intelligent systems programming, and big data management.

At ITU, I have also focused on learning as much about programming as is possible in my two years. While the main focus of most courses have primarily been Java, I have also aquainted myself with Scala through the course Advanced Programming, which also focused on functional programming.

Outside of Java and Scala, I have taught myself Python in my spare time, and I find it an interesting change from Java and Scala. I have mainly used it for smaller programs/scripts, but also for my website development which I do in Python with the Django framework. Examples of websites I have written with Django include gaardlauget-christianshavn and this site. Common for both sites is that content -- news posts, blog posts, media -- can be added dynamically through an admin interface.

I have a bachelor degree from Copenhagen Business School in Business, Asian Language and Culture where my focus was on Japan and Japanese language and business culture. During the bachelor work, I spent a semester studying at Rikkyo University in Tokyo, Japan. As one result of my studies in Japan, I speak Japanese on a conversational level. Both my studies have been completely in English.

After acquiring my bachelor degree I decided to pursue a software development path because I have a passion for both business and information technology. I would like to see myself as a connecting element between the two.

I'm currently employed as a student assistant in ReportSoft, where my main responsibilities are helping the developers with various tasks such as writing tests for web applications, and developing financial report content and templates for clients. I have worked with tools such as Scala (Slick, Selenium), JavaScript, BIRT (Business Intelligence Reporting Tool), and others.
